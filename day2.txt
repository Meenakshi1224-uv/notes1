How the Test Works
The classic setup, which Turing called the "Imitation Game," involves three participants:
	1. A Human Evaluator: A judge who engages in a text-based conversation with two parties.
	2. A Human Participant: A real person providing natural responses.
	3. A Machine: An AI designed to mimic human conversation.
The judge is separated from the other two and does not know which is which. If the judge cannot reliably tell the machine from the human based on the conversation, the machine is said to have passed the test.
